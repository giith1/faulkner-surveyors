What is a Party Wall Agreement?

A party wall agreement is a formal legal document that defines the rights and obligations of property owners who share a common boundary or structure.

It primarily deals with matters related to construction activities that could affect the shared wall or structure, including repairs, maintenance, and modifications.

These agreements are often required when construction work is expected to impact a shared wall, and in some cases, they may also apply to boundary line extensions, depending on the specific situation.

What is a party wall?

A party wall is a common wall shared by two properties, serving as a divider between them. It may also refer to a wall that sits along the boundary line separating two properties.

According to the Party Wall etc. Act 1996, a party wall is defined as a wall, fence, or other structure that acts as a boundary between two or more properties.
