What is a Party Wall Agreement?

A party wall agreement is a legally binding document between property owners that defines the rights and obligations of each party when undertaking construction work involving a shared wall or structure.

Its primary aim is to safeguard the interests of all involved and to minimize the likelihood of disputes between neighbors.

When is a Party Wall Agreement Needed?

A party wall agreement is necessary when undertaking specific types of construction work, including: constructing a new wall adjacent to a neighboring property, cutting into an existing wall to insert a beam or support, or excavating near a neighboring property.

If these activities could impact the structure or stability of a shared wall, obtaining a party wall agreement is required before commencing the work.

HOW WE WORK

Many homeowners remain unaware of the Party Wall Act of 1996, which requires property owners to issue a party wall notice before undertaking certain types of construction, including:

  • Building on or near the boundary line
  • Modifying or working on a party wall or structure
  • Excavating close to a neighboring property

As the property owner planning such work, it’s essential to understand your rights and responsibilities under the Act to safeguard the interests of Adjoining Owners.

As your Party Wall Surveyor, our role is to thoroughly review the proposed works and ensure they are designed, executed, and completed in a way that prevents damage to neighboring properties. In the unlikely event of damage, we will facilitate compensation for the affected Adjoining Owners, as their rights are protected under the Act.

The Party Wall Act also provides surveyors from both properties with rights of access to carry out necessary inspections, ensuring the project complies with legal requirements.

HOW TO OBTAIN A
PARTY WALL
AGREEMENT?

The process of obtaining a party wall agreement begins with serving a formal notice to your neighbors.

This notice should outline the details of the intended work and specify the planned start date.

Neighbors have 14 days to respond to the notice.

If they fail to respond within this period, they are considered to have dissented, triggering the initiation of a dispute resolution process.

In cases where neighbors object to the proposed work, it may be necessary to engage a surveyor to mediate between the parties.

The surveyor will draft a party wall award, a legally binding document that defines the rights and obligations of each party involved.
